## The Origins: Pre-Bitcoin Digital Cash Attempts {#origins}

Bitcoin didn't emerge from nothing. It solved problems that decades of cryptographers, cypherpunks, and computer scientists had wrestled with:

**Pre-Bitcoin digital cash experiments:**

| Project | Year | Creator | Problem |
|---------|------|---------|---------|
| DigiCash | 1989 | David Chaum | Centralized — required company to operate |
| b-money | 1998 | Wei Dai | Never implemented |
| Bit Gold | 1998 | Nick Szabo | Double-spend problem unsolved |
| RPOW | 2004 | Hal Finney | Required central server |
| e-gold | 1996 | Douglas Jackson | Centralized, shut down by government |

The central problem was the **double-spend problem**: how do you prevent someone from spending the same digital money twice without a trusted central party keeping records?

Bitcoin solved it for the first time in history.

> **Featured Snippet Answer:** Bitcoin was created in 2008 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto, who published a whitepaper titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System." The genesis block was mined on January 3, 2009. Bitcoin grew from a cryptographer's experiment to a trillion-dollar global asset class, surviving exchange hacks, regulatory battles, multiple market cycles, and emerging stronger each time.

## 2008: The Whitepaper That Changed Finance {#whitepaper}

On **October 31, 2008** — during the depths of the global financial crisis — an anonymous individual or group using the name **Satoshi Nakamoto** published a 9-page paper to a cryptography mailing list:

**"Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System"**

The timing was not coincidental. The paper appeared weeks after Lehman Brothers collapsed and governments began bailouts of failed financial institutions. Bitcoin's opening message was encoded directly into the genesis block:

> *"The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks"*

This headline — etched permanently into Bitcoin's blockchain — signals Bitcoin's birth as a response to centralized financial failure.

Learn more about the mystery surrounding [who is Satoshi Nakamoto](/en/blog/who-is-satoshi-nakamoto/).

The whitepaper introduced:
- **Blockchain**: A chain of cryptographically linked blocks
- **Proof of Work**: Mining as Sybil resistance
- **Decentralization**: No trusted third party required
- **Fixed Supply**: 21 million BTC cap

## 2009: Bitcoin Goes Live {#2009}

**January 3, 2009** — Satoshi Nakamoto mines the **Genesis Block** (Block #0), creating the first 50 BTC.

**January 9, 2009** — Bitcoin software (version 0.1) released open-source to the world.

**January 12, 2009** — First ever Bitcoin transaction: Satoshi sends **10 BTC** to cryptographer **Hal Finney** (widely considered the first person after Satoshi to run Bitcoin).

The real-world value of Bitcoin for most of 2009? **$0.00** — it was an experiment shared between a tiny group of cryptography enthusiasts.

## 2010–2012: The Early Adopter Era {#early-adopters}

### The Bitcoin Pizza — A Legendary Transaction

**May 22, 2010** — Laszlo Hanyecz pays **10,000 BTC** for two Papa John's pizzas, negotiated via the Bitcoin Talk forum. At 2026 prices, those pizzas cost over **$1 billion**.

This date is now celebrated globally as **Bitcoin Pizza Day** — the first documented commercial Bitcoin transaction.

### Bitcoin Gets a Price

By early 2010, the first exchanges (Bitcoin Market, then Mt. Gox) allowed Bitcoin to be traded for dollars. The first quoted price: **$0.003** per BTC.

### 2011: Bitcoin Reaches $1

In February 2011, Bitcoin achieved **price parity with the US Dollar** for the first time. By June 2011, it hit **$31** — followed by its first major crash to $2.

This pattern — explosive rise, spectaular crash, recovery to higher levels — would repeat throughout Bitcoin's history.

### Silk Road (2011–2013)
The dark web marketplace Silk Road began accepting Bitcoin, creating significant early demand but also fueling the narrative that Bitcoin was "criminal money" — a [myth that persists](/en/blog/bitcoin-myths-misconceptions/) despite being thoroughly debunked.

## 2013–2014: The Mt. Gox Era {#mtgox}

**Mt. Gox** (originally a Magic: The Gathering card exchange) became the dominant Bitcoin exchange, handling 70%+ of all global BTC trading at its peak.

**Bitcoin's first major bull run:**
- January 2013: $13
- April 2013: $266 (then crashes to $50)
- November 2013: **$1,000+** — triggered by China's growing interest

**February 2014: The Mt. Gox Collapse**

Mt. Gox filed for bankruptcy, revealing that **850,000 Bitcoin** (worth ~$450 million at the time) had been stolen or lost over years of negligent security. This became the largest exchange hack in history.

**Market impact:** Bitcoin fell from ~$1,000 to ~$300 over the following year.

**Legacy:** Mt. Gox validated the phrase "not your keys, not your coins" and accelerated hardware wallet adoption. It took until 2024 for Mt. Gox creditors to receive some repayment.

---

## 2015–2016: Bitcoin Grows Up {#growth}

Despite the Mt. Gox disaster, Bitcoin development accelerated:

- **2015:** Ethereum launched, expanding the blockchain ecosystem. Bitcoin developers began debating scaling solutions.
- **2015:** Bitcoin price recovered to ~$400
- **2016:** **Second halving** (July) — block reward dropped from 25 to 12.5 BTC
- **2016:** The **Segregated Witness (SegWit)** proposal gained traction, aimed at fixing transaction malleability and scaling Bitcoin

The **Bitcoin vs. Bitcoin Cash scaling war** began during this period, as different factions debated whether to increase block sizes or scale via layer 2 solutions. This eventually led to a [hard fork](/en/blog/bitcoin-forks/) in 2017.

---

## 2017: The ICO Boom and First Mainstream Attention {#2017}

2017 was Bitcoin's coming-out party to mainstream culture:

- **August 2017:** Bitcoin Cash hard fork creates BCH (block size debate)
- **November 2017:** Bitcoin breaks $10,000 for the first time
- **December 17, 2017:** Bitcoin reaches its prior all-time high: **$19,783**
- **December 2017:** Bitcoin futures launched on CME and CBOE

The ICO (Initial Coin Offering) boom drove billions into the crypto market. Media coverage reached fever pitch. "Bitcoin" became a household word globally.

---

## 2018–2019: The Crypto Winter {#crypto-winter}

After the December 2017 peak, markets collapsed:

- **2018:** Bitcoin fell from $19,783 to **$3,200** — a 84% decline
- Most ICO tokens lost 90–99% of their value
- Hundreds of fraudulent projects were exposed as scams

This period became the "Crypto Winter" — but development didn't stop. The Lightning Network, SegWit adoption, and institutional infrastructure were built during the bear market. [Lightning Network](/en/blog/lightning-network/) was launched in 2018 with real-world payment capability.

---

## 2020–2021: Institutional Adoption and New ATHs {#institutional}

**The COVID catalyst:** March 2020 saw Bitcoin crash to $3,800 alongside global markets. Then something unprecedented happened.

**MicroStrategy** (led by Michael Saylor) announced in August 2020 that it would hold Bitcoin as its primary treasury reserve asset — the first publicly traded company to do so.

**Square**, **Tesla**, **PayPal**, and dozens of corporations followed.

The **3rd halving** (May 2020) reduced supply to 6.25 BTC/block.

**Timeline:**
- March 2020: $3,800
- December 2020: $29,000
- April 2021: $64,863 (new ATH)
- November 2021: **$69,000** (new all-time high)

Bitcoin officially joined the $1 trillion market cap club.

---

## 2022: FTX Collapse and the Bear Market {#2022}

**November 2022** marked the most traumatic event since Mt. Gox:

**FTX**, led by Sam Bankman-Fried, collapsed in under a week after revelations that customer funds were being used for risky trades by Alameda Research. $8 billion in customer funds evaporated.

- Bitcoin fell from $21,000 to **$15,500**
- Contagion hit Celsius, Voyager, BlockFi, Three Arrows Capital
- Regulatory backlash intensified globally

**Silver lining:** Bitcoin's actual protocol was unaffected. No code was changed. The on-chain Bitcoin network never missed a block throughout the FTX crisis — validating that Bitcoin's decentralized architecture makes it immune to single-point-of-failure collapses.

## 2023–2024: ETF Era Begins {#etf-era}

**January 10, 2024:** After years of rejections, the SEC approved **11 Bitcoin Spot ETFs** including from BlackRock, Fidelity, and Invesco.

**Impact:** $12+ billion flowed into ETFs within the first quarter — the fastest ETF launch in Wall Street history.

**April 2024:** The **4th halving** at Block 840,000 reduced block reward to 3.125 BTC.

**December 2024:** Bitcoin surpassed **$100,000** for the first time — a 16-year journey from internet experiment to six-figure global reserve asset.

## 2025–2026: Bitcoin at Scale {#present}

As of 2026, Bitcoin has:
- **$1.5+ trillion** market capitalization
- **~57 million** Bitcoin holders globally
- **18,000+** active full nodes
- Policy integration in **El Salvador**, **USA** (Strategic Reserve), **Bhutan**, and others
- [Lightning Network](/en/blog/lightning-network/) handling millions of micropayments daily

The conversation has shifted from "Is Bitcoin real?" to "How does Bitcoin fit into the global financial system?"

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faqs}

### Who invented Bitcoin?
Bitcoin was invented by **Satoshi Nakamoto** — a pseudonymous person or group whose true identity remains unknown. Satoshi published the whitepaper in 2008 and was active until 2010, then disappeared. Learn more in our article [Who Is Satoshi Nakamoto?](/en/blog/who-is-satoshi-nakamoto/)

### When was Bitcoin created?
The Bitcoin whitepaper was published **October 31, 2008**. The Genesis Block was mined **January 3, 2009**. The software was released publicly on **January 9, 2009**.

### Has Bitcoin ever died?
Bitcoin has been declared dead over 400 times by media since 2010. Yet it has consistently recovered from every bear market to new all-time highs. As the saying goes: "Bitcoin is the most resilient protocol in the history of technology."

### What was the first major Bitcoin exchange?
**Mt. Gox** was the first major exchange, founded in 2010. It collapsed in 2014 after 850,000 BTC were stolen.

### Is Bitcoin still growing?
Yes — by every measurable metric: adoption, institutional holdings, developer activity, Lightning Network usage, and regulatory clarity. Bitcoin is in the early-to-mid phase of global adoption.
