## The Philosophy of Self-Custody (Definition) <a id="definition"></a>

Bitcoin is unique because it allows you to be your own bank. In the legacy financial system, you are an "unsecured creditor" to your bank. In Bitcoin, you are the **absolute owner** through the possession of cryptographic keys.

Self-custody is not just a preference; it is the reason Bitcoin exists. Without it, you are simply trading a digital IOU.

---

## Hot vs. Cold Storage (The Fundamental Split) <a id="hot-vs-cold"></a>

Understanding the difference between "Hot" and "Cold" is the first step in any security plan.



- **Hot Wallets:** Best for small amounts of spending money. Like a physical wallet you carry in your pocket.
- **Cold Wallets:** Best for life savings. Like a high-security vault buried in a mountain.

---

## The Seed Phrase: Your Master Key (Security) <a id="seed-phrase"></a>

When you set up a secure wallet, it will give you 12 or 24 words. This is **BIP-39**. 

**The Golden Rules of Seed Phrases:**
1.  **Never take a picture of it.**
2.  **Never type it into a computer or cloud storage.**
3.  **Never say it out loud near a smart speaker (Alexa/Siri).**
4.  **Always store it in a physical, fireproof location.**

If you have these words, you can recreate your wallet anywhere in the world, even if your hardware wallet is smashed, lost, or stolen.

## Hardware Wallets: The Gold Standard (Hardware) <a id="hardware"></a>

A hardware wallet is a dedicated computer designed with one purpose: to sign transactions without ever revealing the private key to your PC or the internet.

## Steel Backups: Protecting Against Disaster (Backup) <a id="steel"></a>

Paper is vulnerable to fire, water, and time. If you are storing significant wealth, your seed phrase should be etched in steel.

**Why Steel?** 
Stainless steel or titanium can survive house fires (up to 1400°C), whereas paper turns to ash at 233°C. Popular solutions include the **Cryptosteel Capsule**, **Billfodl**, and the **Cryptotag Zeus**.

## Multisig: Eliminating Single Points of Failure (Advanced) <a id="multisig"></a>

Even with a hardware wallet, you still have a "Single Point of Failure": if your seed phrase is stolen, your coins are gone. **Multisig (Multi-Signature)** solves this by requiring 2-of-3 or 3-of-5 keys to move money.

**Example 2-of-3 Setup:**
- **Key 1:** At your home.
- **Key 2:** In a safety deposit box or at your office.
- **Key 3:** Held by a trusted third-party service (like Casa or Unchained Capital).

An attacker would need to physically breach two separate locations to steal your funds.

## Operational Security (OpSec) & Inheritance <a id="opsec"></a>

Common mistakes often bypass even the best technical security:
- **Boat Accidents:** Don't tell people how much Bitcoin you have.
- **Wrench Attacks:** Physical security matters. Large holders should use a "Passphrase" (25th word) to create a hidden wallet with the same seed.
- **The "Hit by a Bus" Plan:** Ensure your family knows how to access your Bitcoin if you are no longer there. Use a "Dead Man's Switch" or simple instructions stored with your executor.

## Frequently Asked Questions <a id="faqs"></a>

### 1. Can my hardware wallet be hacked?
Technically, no computer is 100% unhackable, but hardware wallets are designed to withstand physical attacks. To be hacked remotely, an attacker would need to trick you into entering your seed phrase into a fake website. **Your biggest threat is not a hacker; it is your own behavior.**

### 2. Is a paper wallet still a good idea?
No. Paper wallets (generated on a computer and printed) were common in 2013-2015 but are now considered dangerous. They are prone to errors during creation and "change address" issues during spending. Modern hardware wallets are vastly superior.

### 3. What is the 'not your keys, not your coins' rule?
It refers to the fact that if a third party (like an exchange) controls the private keys, they are the technical owners of the Bitcoin. You only have a promise that they will give it back. If they go bust, you lose everything.
