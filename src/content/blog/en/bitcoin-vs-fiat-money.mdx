## 🎯 Introduction: The Great Monetary Divergence

For the first time in history, the world has two competing systems of value. One is based on **Government Decree (Fiat)** and human decision-making; the other is based on **Cryptographic Proof (Bitcoin)** and mathematical consensus. In 2026, as global inflation challenges the stability of the dollar and the euro, understanding the fundamental physics of these two types of "money" is essential for protecting your purchasing power.

## Section 1: Definition & The Trust Paradox

All money is a technology for transporting value across time and space. The difference is the "Anchor."

<DefinitionBlock term="Hard Money vs. Easy Money" icon="⚖️">
  **Hard Money** (like Gold or Bitcoin) is an asset that is physically or mathematically difficult to produce. This ensures it retains value over time. **Easy Money** (Fiat) is an asset that can be created with a keystroke at almost zero cost. History shows that whenever a society switches to easy money, the currency eventually devalues as the supply expands toward infinity.
</DefinitionBlock>

Fiat derives its value from the "Law"; Bitcoin derives its value from the "Code."

## Section 7: FAQ (The CBDC Paradox)

### 1. Are CBDCs just 'digital Bitcoin'?
No. A Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) is the polar opposite of Bitcoin. While Bitcoin is decentralized and private, a CBDC is **centralized and programmable.** A CBDC allows a government to track every transaction, set "expiry dates" on your savings, or block you from buying certain goods.

### 2. Can Bitcoin fail if the internet goes down?
If the entire global internet goes down forever, the world has bigger problems than money. However, Bitcoin can already be broadcast via **Satellite (Blockstream)** and radio waves. As long as there is a way to transmit a few bytes of data, the network survives.

### 3. Why hasn't everyone switched to Bitcoin yet?
Monetization takes time. Bitcoin is transitioning from a "High-Risk Speculation" (2010s) to a "Store of Value" (2020s). Most people only switch systems when the pain of the old system (inflation/censorship) becomes greater than the friction of learning the new one.
