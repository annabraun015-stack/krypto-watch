## 🎯 Introduction: Speed of Light Finance

In 2026, Bitcoin's "Layer 1" (the main blockchain) has become the supreme global settlement layer—the digital equivalent of a massive gold vault. But you don't go to a gold vault to buy a coffee. The **Lightning Network** is the high-speed city built on top of that vault. It allows Bitcoin to scale to billions of daily transactions, settling in milliseconds for a fraction of a cent.

---

## Section 1: Definition & The Layered Approach

Understanding why Bitcoin needs a "second floor."

<DefinitionBlock term="Layer 2 Scaling" icon="🏗️">
  **Layer 2** refers to secondary protocols built *on top* of a blockchain. The Lightning Network uses the security of the Bitcoin blockchain (Layer 1) to settle disputes, but it processes the actual payments off-chain. This allows for nearly infinite speed without bloating the permanent public ledger.
</DefinitionBlock>

By moving small payments off the main chain, Lightning preserves Bitcoin’s decentralization while enabling global commerce.

## Section 7: FAQ (Risks & Realities)

### 1. Can someone steal my money on Lightning?
If you use a **Non-Custodial** wallet (like Phoenix or Breez), your funds are protected by smart contracts. The only risk is if you go offline for months while a malicious channel partner tries to close the channel with an old state. In 2026, **Watchtowers** automatically prevent this for you.

### 2. Why are some Lightning wallets 'Custodial'?
Custodial wallets (like Wallet of Satoshi) are easier for beginners because the company manages the channels and liquidity for you. However, this means they hold your keys. For true security, always migrate to a non-custodial wallet once you are comfortable.

### 3. What is 'Liquidity' in Lightning?
Think of a channel like a tube with balls inside. To receive money, the balls must be on the sender's side. If all the balls are already on your side, you have no "Inbound Liquidity" and cannot receive more. Managing this flow is the main technical task for Lightning users.
