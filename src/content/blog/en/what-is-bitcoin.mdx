## 🎯 Introduction: A New Paradigm for Money

Imagine a world where you can send any amount of money to anyone across the globe instantly, for a fraction of a cent, without asking permission from a bank or a government. This is not science fiction—it is **Bitcoin**. Launched in 2009 by the anonymous [Satoshi Nakamoto](/en/blog/who-is-satoshi-nakamoto/), Bitcoin represents the first time in history that humans have created a purely digital, decentralized, and scarcitied asset.

---

## Section 1: Definition & Overview

At its simplest level, Bitcoin is a digital protocol for the transfer of value. Unlike a credit card payment which relies on a "trusted" third party to verify the balance, Bitcoin relies on mathematics and physics.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin (BTC)" icon="₿">
  **Bitcoin** is a decentralized, peer-to-peer digital currency that uses a public ledger (the Blockchain) to record transactions. It is "Permissionless," meaning anyone can join the network, and "Immutable," meaning once a transaction is recorded, it can never be erased or altered.
</DefinitionBlock>

Why does this matter? For the first time, we have a financial system with no "Single point of failure." There is no head office to raid, no server to shut down, and no CEO to arrest.

## Section 5: Practical Use Cases & Adoption

In 2026, Bitcoin utility has moved beyond speculation:

## Section 7: FAQs (Understanding the Details)

### 1. Is Bitcoin legal?
Yes, in almost all democratic nations. In 2026, most countries have moved away from bans and toward regulating Bitcoin as a legal commodity or "Digital Property."

### 2. Can Bitcoin be hacked?
The Bitcoin network itself has never been hacked. Its security is guaranteed by the combined electricity of millions of miners worldwide (Proof-of-Work). Individual *wallets* can be hacked, but only if the user mismanages their [private keys](/en/blog/public-private-keys/).

### 3. What is a "Satoshi"?
A Satoshi (or "Sat") is the smallest unit of a Bitcoin. One Bitcoin can be divided into 100,000,000 Satoshis, allowing for tiny payments even if the price of 1 BTC is very high.

### 4. Who controls Bitcoin?
No one. Bitcoin is governed by a set of rules (the protocol) that can only be changed if the majority of independent [Node operators](/en/blog/what-is-a-bitcoin-node/) agree. It is the first truly leaderless financial system.
