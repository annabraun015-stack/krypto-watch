## Digital Scarcity in Motion (Definition) {#definition}

In the world of traditional finance, central banks can print an infinite amount of money. In the world of Bitcoin, the rules are written in code. The **Halving** is the mechanism that ensures Bitcoin remains "Hard Money."

Every 210,000 blocks (roughly every 4 years), the number of new Bitcoins created per block is cut exactly in half. This process continues until the year 2140, when the last Satoshi is mined.

> **Featured Snippet Answer:** The Bitcoin Halving is a pre-programmed protocol event that reduces the block reward for miners by 50% every 210,000 blocks. This effectively lowers Bitcoin's annual inflation rate. The most recent halving in April 2024 reduced the reward from 6.25 to 3.125 BTC. By reducing the influx of new supply, the halving historically acts as a catalyst for long-term price appreciation, provided demand remains constant or increases.

*Semantic Triples:*
- **Halving Event** → reduces → **Daily Block Issuance**
- **Block Issuance** → determines → **Bitcoin Inflation Rate**
- **21 Million Cap** → is enforced by → **Halving Schedule**

## How the Halving Works: The Mathematical Engine (Technology) {#math}

The halving is simple but powerful. It is located in the `GetBlockSubsidy` function of the Bitcoin Core source code.

<div class="mermaid">
graph LR
    A[New Block Found] --> B{Blocks < 210,000?}
    B -- Yes --> C[Reward: 50 BTC]
    B -- No --> D{Blocks < 420,000?}
    D -- Yes --> E[Reward: 25 BTC]
    D -- No --> F[Continue Halving Process]
    F --> G[End Supply at 21M]
</div>

This predictable reduction makes Bitcoin the first asset in human history with a supply that is **perfectly inelastic** to price. Even if the price of Bitcoin goes to $1,000,000, the network cannot produce more than the schedule allows.

## The 2024 Halving: The ETF Era (Case Study) {#2024-halving}

The April 2024 halving was unique. For the first time, Bitcoin hit a new All-Time High **before** the halving occurred. 

The introduction of **Spot Bitcoin ETFs** created a "Demand Shock" that met the "Supply Shock" months earlier than usual. In 2026, we see the results: Bitcoin has solidified its place as a "Strategic Reserve Asset" for corporations and even small nation-states.

## Mining Economics: Survival of the Fittest (Industry) {#mining}

When rewards are cut in half, the "weak" miners — those with high electricity costs or inefficient hardware — are forced to shut down. 

- **Hashrate Volatility:** Directly after a halving, the network hashrate often dips as less efficient miners leave.
- **Difficulty Adjustment:** The network automatically adjusts every 2 weeks to ensure blocks are still found every 10 minutes, regardless of how many miners remain.
- **The Shift to Fees:** As block rewards decrease, miners rely more and more on **Transaction Fees** from the [Lightning Network](/en/blog/lightning-network/) and on-chain activity.

## The Stock-to-Flow Model and Scarcity (Economics) {#s2f}

The **Stock-to-Flow (S2F)** ratio is a way to measure the scarcity of a resource. It is the total existing supply divided by the annual new supply.

| Asset | S2F Ratio (Estimated) | Note |
|-------|-----------------------|------|
| **Silver** | ~22 | Used industrially, consumed. |
| **Gold** | ~60 | Hardest physical asset. |
| **Bitcoin (Post-2024)** | **~120** | **Twice as scarce as gold.** |

By 2028, Bitcoin's S2F ratio will double again, making it mathematically the "Hardest" asset ever known to humanity.

## What Happens After 2140? (Long-Term Future) {#future}

Critics often ask: "Will miners stop securing the network when the reward hits zero?" 

In a mature Bitcoin economy, the block reward is replaced by a robust **Fee Market.** High-value on-chain settlemet and millions of Layer 2 transactions provide enough revenue to keep miners hashing. By 2140, Bitcoin will have had over 130 years to prove this model viable.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faqs}

### 1. Does the halving happen on a specific date?
No. The halving is based on "Block Height." It happens exactly every 210,000 blocks. While we can estimate the date (roughly every 4 years), a faster or slower network hashrate can move the actual date by weeks.

### 2. Is there a "Last Bitcoin"?
Yes, the very last fraction of a Bitcoin will be mined around the year 2140. After that, the supply is capped forever at 21,000,000.00000000 BTC. 

### 3. Will the 21 million limit ever be changed?
To change the limit, you would need to convince 100% of nodes and miners to agree to devalue their own holdings. Technically it is possible, but socially and economically it is virtually impossible in the current decentralized structure.
