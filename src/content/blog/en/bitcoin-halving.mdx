## What Is Bitcoin Halving? {#definition}

**Bitcoin halving** is an event encoded into Bitcoin's protocol that cuts the block reward paid to miners exactly in half, occurring approximately every **210,000 blocks** — roughly every 4 years.

It's one of Bitcoin's most important monetary policy mechanisms, ensuring that new BTC enters circulation at a predictably decreasing rate until the total supply reaches 21 million.

> **Featured Snippet Answer:** Bitcoin halving is a programmatic event that reduces miners' block rewards by 50% every 210,000 blocks (~4 years). It controls Bitcoin's inflation rate, making BTC progressively scarcer over time. The most recent halving occurred in April 2024, reducing the block reward from 6.25 BTC to 3.125 BTC. The next halving is expected around 2028.

Halving is a core part of [why Bitcoin's supply is limited to 21 million](/en/blog/bitcoin-supply-21-million/) and a major factor in [what determines Bitcoin's price](/en/blog/what-determines-bitcoins-price/).

## Full Halving History: 2009–2028 {#history}

| Event | Date | Block Height | Block Reward | Circulating Supply |
|-------|------|-------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Genesis Block | Jan 2009 | 0 | 50 BTC | ~0 BTC |
| 1st Halving | Nov 2012 | 210,000 | 25 BTC | ~10.5M BTC |
| 2nd Halving | Jul 2016 | 420,000 | 12.5 BTC | ~15.75M BTC |
| 3rd Halving | May 2020 | 630,000 | 6.25 BTC | ~18.37M BTC |
| 4th Halving | Apr 2024 | 840,000 | 3.125 BTC | ~19.7M BTC |

As you can see, over **93% of all Bitcoin** has already been mined as of 2026. The remaining ~1.3 million BTC will be mined over the next 114 years.

## Why Does Halving Happen? {#why}

Satoshi Nakamoto designed the halving schedule to create **programmatic monetary scarcity** — a disinflationary issuance rate that can never be tampered with.

### Bitcoin's Inflation Schedule

```
Bitcoin Annual Inflation Rate:
2009: ~50% (lots of new BTC entering supply)
2012: ~25%
2016: ~12%
2020: ~6.25%
2024: ~3.125% → below gold's annual supply growth!
2028: ~1.56%
2032: ~0.78%
...approaching zero by 2140
```

Compare this to fiat currencies where central banks can print unlimited money, causing inflation. Bitcoin's halving schedule makes it the hardest money ever created.

This contrast is examined in depth in our article on [Bitcoin vs. Fiat Money](/en/blog/bitcoin-vs-fiat-money/).

## The 2024 Bitcoin Halving: What Happened {#2024-halving}

The **4th Bitcoin halving** occurred at block **840,000** on **April 19, 2024** at approximately 00:09 UTC.

### Key Facts:
- Block reward dropped from **6.25 BTC → 3.125 BTC**
- Daily new BTC supply decreased from ~900 BTC/day → ~450 BTC/day
- Bitcoin price at halving: **~$63,800**
- Bitcoin ETFs had already absorbed over $12 billion in the preceding 3 months

### What Made 2024 Different From Previous Halvings

The 2024 halving occurred against an unprecedented backdrop:
- **Bitcoin ETFs** (BlackRock IBIT, Fidelity FBTC) were already live and buying 3–4x daily mined supply
- **Institutional demand** far outpaced the reduced supply
- The supply shock was amplified by ETF-driven structural demand

Within 6 months of the halving, Bitcoin reached a **new all-time high above $100,000** — the fastest ATH progression after any halving.

## How Halving Affects Bitcoin's Price {#price-impact}

Bitcoin halvings have historically preceded major price appreciation through a simple supply-demand dynamic:

```
Halving → Less New Supply → Same or Growing Demand → Higher Price
```

### Price Performance After Each Halving

| Halving | Pre-Halving Price | 12-Month ATH After | Return |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|--------|
| 2012 | ~$12 | $1,100 | +9,066% |
| 2016 | ~$650 | $20,000 | +2,977% |
| 2020 | ~$8,600 | $69,000 | +702% |
| 2024 | ~$63,800 | $108,000+ | +69%+ (ongoing) |

**Returns are diminishing** as Bitcoin's market cap grows — but the direction has been consistent. [Bitcoin's price dynamics](/en/blog/what-determines-bitcoins-price/) are complex, but supply reduction is a foundational force.

### The Stock-to-Flow (S2F) Model {#s2f}

Analyst PlanB's **Stock-to-Flow model** measures scarcity by dividing total supply (stock) by annual production (flow):

| Asset | Stock-to-Flow Ratio |
|-------|-------------------|
| Silver | ~22 |
| Gold | ~62 |
| Bitcoin (post-2024) | ~120 |

A higher ratio = greater scarcity. Bitcoin surpassed gold's S2F ratio after the 2024 halving, making it mathematically the scarcest major asset on Earth.

*Note: S2F is a model, not a guarantee. Market dynamics, regulation, and macroeconomics also play major roles.*

## What Happens When the Last Bitcoin Is Mined? {#last-bitcoin}

Around the year **2140**, the final fraction of a Bitcoin (a satoshi — 0.00000001 BTC) will be mined. After this:

- No new BTC enters circulation
- Miners are compensated **entirely from transaction fees**
- Bitcoin becomes a fully fixed-supply asset

The [Bitcoin fee market](/en/blog/bitcoin-transaction-fees/) will need to generate sufficient revenue to incentivize miners to continue securing the network. This is an open question in Bitcoin's long-term security model — but with [Lightning Network](/en/blog/lightning-network/) micropayments and growing on-chain volume, the fee market is expected to mature significantly.

## Mining Profitability After Halving {#mining-economics}

Halvings are brutal for inefficient miners. Less revenue per block means marginal operations become unprofitable and must shut down.

```
Post-Halving Mining Dynamics:
Revenue cut in half →
Less efficient miners exit →
Hash rate initially drops →
Difficulty adjusts downward →
Remaining miners become more profitable →
New equilibrium established
```

However, if Bitcoin's price rises significantly post-halving (as history suggests), miners with access to cheap electricity remain profitable or even more profitable than before.

Learn more about [how Bitcoin mining works](/en/blog/bitcoin-mining/) and the [role miners play in security](/en/blog/role-of-miners-bitcoin-security/).

## 2028 Halving: What to Expect {#2028}

The **5th Bitcoin halving** is projected for approximately **April 2028** at block 1,050,000.

Key forecasts:
- Block reward will drop to **1.5625 BTC**
- Daily new supply: ~225 BTC/day (down from 450)
- By 2028, Bitcoin ETFs are expected to hold millions of BTC in AUM
- The supply shock could be even more dramatic given expected institutional holdings

The 2028 halving will occur when Bitcoin inflation rate drops below **0.8% annually** — less than half the Federal Reserve's 2% inflation target for the US dollar.

## Frequently Asked Questions {#faqs}

### How often does Bitcoin halving happen?
Every 210,000 blocks, which takes approximately 4 years given Bitcoin's ~10-minute average block time. The exact date varies slightly depending on network hash rate.

### Does halving always increase Bitcoin's price?
Historically yes, but not immediately. Price response typically occurs over 12–18 months. The halving reduces supply; price response depends on whether demand remains sustained.

### Can the halving schedule be changed?
Only with near-universal consensus from all Bitcoin nodes and participants. Any attempt to change the halving schedule would effectively create a hard fork — a different cryptocurrency. Bitcoin Core has shown zero appetite for changes to the issuance schedule.

### What is the current Bitcoin block reward?
As of April 2024, it is **3.125 BTC per block**, paid to the winning miner approximately every 10 minutes.

### How many bitcoins are mined per day in 2026?
Approximately **450 BTC per day** (144 blocks/day × 3.125 BTC/block).
