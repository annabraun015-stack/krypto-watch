## 🎯 Introduction: The Programmatic Heartbeat of Scarcity

The most powerful feature of Bitcoin is not its technology, but its **Monetary Policy**. While central banks around the world change interest rates and print money based on human discretion, Bitcoin operates on a fixed, predictable schedule. This schedule is anchored by the **Halving**—an event that automatically reduces the issuance of new Bitcoin every four years. In 2026, we are living in the post-2024 "ETF Era," where the halving's impact is more profound than ever.

---

## Section 1: Definition & The Purpose of Halving

The halving is the network's way of ensuring that Bitcoin mimics the properties of a physical commodity like gold.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin Halving" icon="⏳">
  A **Bitcoin Halving** is a pre-programmed event that occurs every 210,000 blocks (approximately every 4 years). It cuts the reward given to miners for securing the network in half. This reduces the rate at which new Bitcoin enters the market, ensuring that the total supply never exceeds 21 million and creating a "Supply Shock" that counters inflation.
</DefinitionBlock>

Without the halving, all 21 million Bitcoins would have been mined in the first few years. By spreading the issuance over 130 years, Satoshi Nakamoto created a "Slow Release" mechanism that allows the network to grow in value as it becomes scarcer.

## Section 7: FAQ (Common Investor Myths)

### 1. Will miners quit when the reward is halved?
Some might. This is a "cleaning" of the network. Efficient miners with cheap electricity survive, while inefficient ones fail. The [Difficulty Adjustment](/en/blog/bitcoin-mining/) ensures the network continues regardless.

### 2. Is the halving 'priced in'?
This is the great debate of 2026. While the schedule is known, the **actual impact** of reduced sell-pressure from miners is felt gradually over months. You cannot "price in" a physical reduction of available supply that hasn't happened yet.

### 3. What happens after the last halving in 2140?
Miners will be paid 100% by [Transaction Fees](/en/blog/bitcoin-transaction-fees/). As Bitcoin settles billions in value per day, the competition for space in a block will provide more than enough revenue to maintain network security.
