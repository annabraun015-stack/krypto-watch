## 🎯 Introduction: The Programmatic Heartbeat of Scarcity

The most powerful feature of Bitcoin is not its technology, but its **Monetary Policy**. While central banks around the world change interest rates and print money based on human discretion and political pressure, Bitcoin operates on a fixed, predictable, and immutable schedule. 

This schedule is anchored by the **Halving**—an event that automatically reduces the issuance of new Bitcoin every four years. In 2026, we are living in the high-stakes "Post-2024 ETF Era," where the halving's impact on global liquidity is more profound than ever. It is the heartbeat of the world's first digital sound money system.

---

## Section 1: Definition — Why the Halving Matters

The halving is the primary mechanism that ensures Bitcoin remains a disinflationary asset, eventually becoming perfectly scarce.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin Halving" icon="⏳">
  A **Bitcoin Halving** is a pre-programmed event that occurs every 210,000 blocks (approximately every 4 years). It cuts the reward given to miners for securing the network in half. This reduces the rate at which new Bitcoin enters the market, ensuring that the total supply never exceeds 21 million and creating a "Supply Shock" that counters global fiat inflation.
</DefinitionBlock>

Satoshi Nakamoto designed Bitcoin to mimic the extraction of gold: as time goes on, it becomes 50% harder to "extract" the remaining supply. This spreads the issuance over roughly 130 years, giving the network time to grow in value and security while the supply curve flattens.

## Section 4: History of Scarcity Timeline

Past halvings have proven that Bitcoin's supply shock is the most reliable event in finance.

Bitcoin's journey toward 21 million units is marked by distinct epochs. The Genesis era (2009) began with a 50 BTC reward, which was halved for the first time in 2012 to 25 BTC as the Silk Road era unfolded. The second halving in 2016 reduced rewards to 12.5 BTC during the ICO craze, followed by the 2020 halving to 6.25 BTC as institutions and corporations began to enter the market. The 2024 halving cut the reward further to 3.125 BTC, coinciding with the Spot ETF revolution, and the upcoming 2028 halving will drop it to 1.56 BTC, solidifying Bitcoin's role as a primary strategic reserve asset.

## Section 7: FAQ — The Economic Countdown

### 1. When is the next Bitcoin halving?
Following the 2024 halving, the next event is scheduled for **April 2028**. At that point, the "Block Subsidy" will drop from 3.125 BTC to **1.5625 BTC**. This process will continue until the year 2140 when new issuance ends entirely.

### 2. What happens to the price after a halving?
Historically, the 18 months following a halving have seen the most explosive growth in Bitcoin's price. This is due to the **"Supply Shock"**—when the amount of new Bitcoin available to buy is cut in half, while global demand (especially through ETFs) remains steady or increases.

### 3. Will miners quit after a halving?
Some will. Miners with outdated hardware or expensive electricity may become unprofitable and shut down. However, Bitcoin's **Difficulty Adjustment** ensures that as miners leave, it becomes easier for the remaining miners to find blocks, keeping the network stable and secure.

### 4. Is the halving guaranteed to happen?
Yes. The halving is part of the "Consensus Rules" that every [Bitcoin Node](/en/blog/what-is-a-bitcoin-node/) enforces. It is a mathematical certainty that is not subject to the whims of any government or central bank.

### 5. How many halvings have happened?
As of 2026, the world has seen **four** halving events (2012, 2016, 2020, and 2024). Each one has successfully transitioned Bitcoin into a new epoch of higher scarcity and institutional adoption.
