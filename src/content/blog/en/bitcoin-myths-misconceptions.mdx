## Section 5: The 'Death Spiral' & Difficulty Adjustment

Critics often claim that if the price drops, miners will quit, leading to a "Death Spiral" where no one processes transactions. 
This ignores the **Difficulty Adjustment**. If miners quit, the network automatically makes it easier (and thus more profitable) for the remaining miners to find blocks. Bitcoin is the only machine in the world that automatically repairs its own profitability every 2,000 blocks.

## Section 7: FAQ — Professional FUD Disposal

### 1. What happens if the internet is shut down?
Bitcoin survives. It is already being broadcast via **Satellites** and radio waves. As long as two humans can transmit a few bits of data, the ledger continues. To shut down Bitcoin, you would need to shut down the laws of physics and mathematics.

### 2. Can't another coin do what Bitcoin does, but better?
You can copy the code, but you cannot copy the **Network Effect** or the **Immaculate Conception**. Bitcoin was the only coin launched without a pre-sale, a marketing budget, or a known leader. This "Fair Start" is a one-time event in history that cannot be replicated.

### 3. Isn't Bitcoin too volatile to be 'Real Money'?
Volatility is the price we pay for the world's most accurate "Asset Discovery." Because Bitcoin's supply is perfectly fixed, every increase or decrease in demand translates 1:1 into price. As Bitcoin grows to match the size of the gold market, this volatility will naturally diminish.
