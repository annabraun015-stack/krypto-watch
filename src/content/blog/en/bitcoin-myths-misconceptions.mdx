## Section 7: FAQ (Addressing the FUD)

### 1. If it's so good, why is it so volatile?
Bitcoin is a nascent asset in the process of "Price Discovery." Because its supply is perfectly fixed, any change in demand results purely in price movement. As its market cap grows to gold-level scales, volatility diminishes.

### 2. What if a government 'replaces' it?
Governments can create "CBDCs" (Central Bank Digital Currencies), but these are the opposite of Bitcoin. CBDCs are centralized and allow for more surveillance. Bitcoin is decentralized and provides freedom. They serve entirely different purposes.

### 3. isn't it too late to buy?
This question was asked at $100, $1,000, and $10,000. While the largest gains are in the past, Bitcoin is transitioning from a speculative asset to a global reserve. Many institutions are only just beginning their 10-year allocation plans.
