## 🎯 Introduction: The Price Discovery War

Bitcoin's price is not a random number, nor is it set by a central authority. It is the result of a **24/7, global price discovery mechanism**—a constant tug-of-war between millions of participants. In 2026, as Bitcoin transitions from a retail-driven asset to a sovereign reserve asset, the forces determining its value have become increasingly complex. Understanding these invisible strings is the key to navigating the market without emotion.

---

## Section 1: Definition & The Fixed Supply

Unlike every other asset in human history, Bitcoin's supply side is a mathematical constant.

<DefinitionBlock term="Supply Inelasticity" icon="📐">
  **Supply Inelasticity** means that no matter how much the price rises, the network cannot produce more Bitcoin than the protocol allows (the 10-minute block target). In traditional commodities like Gold, a higher price incentivizes more mining. In Bitcoin, the supply remains **inflexible**, forcing all demand pressure into the price.
</DefinitionBlock>

This is the "Black Hole" effect of Bitcoin economics: it is the only asset where demand cannot be met with more production.

## Section 7: FAQ (Volatility & Control)

### 1. Do 'Whales' control the price?
In the short term, yes. Large holders can move the price by placing massive sell orders to trigger liquidations. However, over the long term, no whale is large enough to stop the fundamental absorption of Bitcoin by global institutions and sovereign states.

### 2. Why does the price drop during news events?
"Sell the news" is a common trading phenomenon. Often, the market "prices in" an event (like a halving or an ETF approval) months in advance. When the event finally happens, short-term traders sell to lock in profits, leading to a temporary price drop despite the positive news.

### 3. Will Bitcoin's price ever be stable?
Stability is a factor of market capitalization. As Bitcoin's value grows to rival Gold ($14T+), it will require significantly more capital to move the price by 1%. By 2030, Bitcoin is expected to be as stable as major national currencies.
