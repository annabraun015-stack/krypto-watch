## 🎯 Introduction: The Physical Foundation of Digital Money

Many people mistakenly view Bitcoin as a purely "virtual" or "imaginary" asset. In reality, its security and value are anchored in the physical laws of thermodynamics through the process of **Mining**. 

Mining is the mechanism that ties the digital ledger to the objective cost of energy and computing power. In 2026, Bitcoin mining has evolved from a hobbyist's experiment into a critical multi-billion dollar industrial sector that not only secures trillions in global wealth but also stabilizes national electrical grids. It is the bridge between bits and atoms.

---

## Section 1: Definition — The Collective Audit

At its core, mining is not about "creating money"; it is about **Reaching Consensus** in a leaderless system.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin Mining" icon="⚒️">
  **Bitcoin Mining** is the process of using specialized computing hardware (ASICs) to solve complex cryptographic puzzles. This effort verifies pending transactions, bundles them into blocks, and adds them to the immutable public ledger. This "Proof-of-Work" (PoW) makes it physically impossible for any actor to rewrite the history of the network without expending more energy than the rest of the world combined.
</DefinitionBlock>

Why call it "mining"? Much like gold mining requires physical labor and machinery to extract limited value from the earth, Bitcoin mining requires electrical labor and computation to extract units from a limited code-based supply. It is the heartbeat of the network.

## Section 4: Hardware Evolution (ASICs vs. The World)

The race for mining efficiency has driven global semiconductor innovation.

The evolution of mining has seen an exponential increase in both computing scale and efficiency. In the early days of 2009, ordinary CPUs were sufficient to secure the network at kilohash scales. By 2011, the network migrated to GPUs (Graphics Cards) for higher performance, but the real revolution began in 2013 with the introduction of Application-Specific Integrated Circuits (ASICs). In 2026, the industry is dominated by high-tech ASICs using 3nm chips, capable of over 200 Terahashes per second, making any attempt to attack the network economically impossible.

Today, a single high-end ASIC machine is more powerful than the entire network was during its first year of existence. This exponential growth makes an "Attack" on the network economically suicidal.

## Section 7: FAQ — Professional Insights

### 1. Can a government ban Bitcoin mining?
History has proven that "bans" only cause the hashrate to migrate. When China banned mining in 2021, the network recovered fully within months, demonstrating its **Extreme Resilience**. Today, hashrate is more globally distributed than ever.

### 2. Is Bitcoin mining profitable?
Profitability depends on two factors: the local cost of electricity and the efficiency of your hardware. In 2026, large-scale industrial operations dominate the market, though "Home Mining" remains popular in regions with cold climates where mining heat is used for homes.

### 3. What is a 'Mining Pool'?
Since the odds of a single machine finding a block are trillions to one, miners combine their power in "Pools." They share the rewards based on the "Share of Work" each machine contributed, providing a steady and predictable income.

### 4. How long does it take to mine 1 Bitcoin?
As of 2026, the reward for finding a block is **3.125 BTC**. Because the network hashrate is so high, a single high-end ASIC miner might take several decades to find a block alone. This is why almost all miners join a pool to receive small, daily payments proportional to their hardware's power.

### 5. What happens after all 21 million Bitcoins are mined?
Around the year **2140**, the last Satoshi will be mined. At that point, the "Block Subsidy" ends. Miners will then be incentivized entirely by the **Transaction Fees** paid by users. As Bitcoin becomes a global reserve asset, the competition for block space is expected to provide more than enough revenue to keep the network secure.
