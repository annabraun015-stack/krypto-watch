## 🎯 Introduction: The Most Secure Network in History

When critics ask if Bitcoin is "secure," they are often conflating the security of a user (who might lose their password) with the security of the network (which protects trillions of dollars). In 2026, Bitcoin represents the most robust, decentralized computing network ever constructed by mankind. Its defenses are built not of steel, but of **Mathematics** and **Energy**.

---

## Section 1: Definition & The Three Layers of Security

Bitcoin security is a multi-layered concept. It is helpful to visualize it as a fortress protected by physical laws rather than corporate firewalls.

<DefinitionBlock term="Network Security" icon="🛡️">
  **Bitcoin Security** refers to the protocol's ability to resist double-spending, forgery, and censorship. It is maintained by a global consensus of miners (providing energy) and nodes (verifying rules). This decentralized structure ensures that no single company, government, or hacker can control the system.
</DefinitionBlock>

The three pillars of this security are:
1. **Cryptography:** Ensuring only the owner of a key can move funds.
2. **Decentralization:** Distributing the ledger across 18,000+ independent [Nodes](/en/blog/what-is-a-bitcoin-node/).
3. **Proof-of-Work:** Making it astronomically expensive to lie to the network.

## Section 7: FAQ (Security Paradoxes)

### 1. Has Bitcoin ever been hacked?
No. Centralized exchanges like FTX or Mt. Gox have been "hacked" (or defrauded), but the Bitcoin protocol itself has a 99.99% uptime and zero successful breaches since 2009.

### 2. Can quantum computers break Bitcoin?
Not today. While quantum computers could theoretically solve the math behind the keys, current machines are decades away from the necessary power. If they do arrive, Bitcoin will upgrade to "Post-Quantum Cryptography" through a simple software update.

### 3. Is my Bitcoin safe on an exchange?
No. An exchange is a centralized database. If the exchange is hacked or goes bankrupt, your funds are at risk. **Self-custody** via a hardware wallet is the only way to be 100% secure.
