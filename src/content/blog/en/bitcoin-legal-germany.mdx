## Section 5: The Legal Difference — Unit of Account vs. Legal Tender

A common myth is that Bitcoin is "illegal" because it isn't the Euro. This is technically incorrect.
- **Euro (Legal Tender):** Must be accepted by law for all debts.
- **Bitcoin (Unit of Account):** Can be accepted by **Contractual Agreement**. 
In Germany, "Freedom of Contract" (Vertragsfreiheit) allows any merchant to accept Bitcoin. Once a price is agreed upon in BTC, that contract is fully enforceable in a German court of law.

<h2 id="faq">Section 7: FAQ — Legal & Tax Clarity</h2>

### 1. Is Bitcoin legal in Germany?
Yes. Bitcoin has been recognized as a **Unit of Account** (Rechnungseinheit) by the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin) since 2013. It is a legal financial instrument that can be used for payments and investments.

### 2. How is Bitcoin taxed in Germany?
Bitcoin is not subject to the 25% flat withholding tax (Abgeltungssteuer) like stocks. Instead, it is treated as a "Private Disposal Transaction." If you hold your Bitcoin for **more than one year**, the profit from the sale is **100% tax-free**, regardless of the amount.

### 3. What is the tax-free limit for short-term gains?
If you sell Bitcoin within the one-year holding period, you have a **"Freigrenze" of €600** per year. If your total crypto profits are below this, you pay zero tax. If you earn €601, you must pay tax on the entire €601 at your personal income tax rate.

### 4. Is Bitcoin mining taxable?
In most cases, the Finanzamt views mining as a **Commercial Activity** (Gewerbliche Tätigkeit). This means you must register a business, pay trade tax (Gewerbesteuer), and you cannot benefit from the one-year tax-free holding rule that applies to private investors.

### 5. Do I have to report Bitcoin to the Finanzamt?
If you are a "HODLer" and hold your coins for more than 12 months, you generally do **not** need to report them on your tax return. However, if you trade frequently or sell within a year, you must keep meticulous records (using tools like Cointracking) to fulfill your reporting obligations.
