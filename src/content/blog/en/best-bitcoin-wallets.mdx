## Section 1: Definition & The Hierarchy of Custody

A "wallet" is a misnomer. It doesn't hold Bitcoin; it holds the authorization code to move it.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin Wallet" icon="🔑">
  A **Bitcoin Wallet** is a tool that manages your Private Keys. The gold standard in 2026 is **Self-Custody**, where you—and only you—possess the keys. The hierarchy of security ranges from "Hot Wallets" (connected to the internet) to "Cold Storage" (physically isolated hardware), ensuring that your wealth is protected according to its value.
</DefinitionBlock>

Remember: *"Not your keys, not your coins."* If you keep your BTC on an exchange, you only own a promise. With a non-custodial wallet, you own the asset.

## Section 7: FAQ (Security Best Practices)

### 1. Is it safe to buy a wallet on Amazon?
No. **Always buy directly from the manufacturer.** Scammers have been known to intercept packages, modify the hardware, or include a "pre-configured" seed phrase to steal your funds the moment you deposit.

### 2. What happens if the wallet company goes bankrupt?
Your Bitcoin is safe. Most hardware wallets use the BIP-39 standard. If Ledger or Trezor ends today, you can take your 24 words and restore your wallet on any other open-source software like Sparrow or Electrum.

### 3. Do I need a different wallet for every coin?
"Multi-currency" hardware wallets can store BTC, ETH, and thousands of others. However, "Bitcoin-only" versions (like BitBox02 BTC-only) are often more secure because they have less code, reducing the "attack surface" for hackers.
