## 🎯 Introduction: The Guardians of the Ledger

In traditional finance, security is maintained by walls, guards, and laws. In Bitcoin, security is maintained by **Miners**. Miners are the specialized entities that ensure the ledger is honest, the history is unchangeable, and the network is decentralized. In 2026, the "Hash Power" provided by miners has become a pillar of global economic stability.

## Section 1: Definition & The Immune System

Mining is not just about "creating new coins"; it is the immune system of the Bitcoin protocol.

<DefinitionBlock term="Miners' Role" icon="🛡️">
  The **Role of Miners** is to provide computational work (Hash Rate) that secures the network against "Double-Spending" and "Reorganization" attacks. By expending real-world energy, miners anchor the digital blockchain in physical reality, making the cost of cheating the system higher than the potential gain.
</DefinitionBlock>

Without miners, Bitcoin would be just a database that anyone could edit. With miners, it is a "One-Way Street" of time and truth.

## Section 7: FAQ (Security Concerns)

### 1. Can miners change the 21 million supply limit?
No. Independent [Nodes](/en/blog/what-is-a-bitcoin-node/) enforce that rule. If a miner tries to print more Bitcoin, the nodes will reject their block, and the miner's costly electricity spent finding that block will be wasted.

### 2. What happens if a government seizes all miners?
Mining is now globally distributed. If the US seized all miners, the network's hash rate would drop, but the [Difficulty would adjust](/en/blog/bitcoin-mining/), making it profitable for miners in other countries (like the UAE or Argentina) to pick up the slack.

### 3. Does mining use too much energy?
Bitcoin uses energy to create an unforgeable settlement layer. Over 50% of this energy is now sourced from renewable or "stranded" methane gas, making it one of the cleanest industrial sectors of its size in 2026.
