## 🎯 Introduction: The Speed of Trust

In traditional banking, "settlement" can take days. In Bitcoin, settlement is measured in **Confirmations**. A confirmation is a mathematical proof that your transaction is now a permanent part of the global history. In 2026, understanding this metric is crucial for anyone accepting Bitcoin for goods or moving significant wealth across the network.

---

## Section 1: Definition & The Block Burial

A confirmation is not just a "check mark"; it is a layer of computational work protecting your funds.

<DefinitionBlock term="Bitcoin Confirmation" icon="📐">
  A **Confirmation** occurs when a [Miner](/en/blog/bitcoin-mining/) includes your transaction in a new block. Every subsequent block added to the chain *after* yours counts as an additional confirmation. Each new layer makes it exponentially more difficult for an attacker to re-write the history and reverse your payment.
</DefinitionBlock>

Think of it like building a house: the first confirmation is the foundation. Every subsequent confirmation is another floor of concrete poured on top. To change the foundation, an attacker would have to destroy the entire building.

## Section 7: FAQ (Stuck Transactions)

### 1. Why is my transaction stuck at 0 confirmations?
This is almost always due to setting a **Fee** that is too low. In periods of high congestion, miners will ignore low-paying transactions. You can use **RBF (Replace-By-Fee)** to increase the fee and get confirmed.

### 2. Can a transaction be 'unconfirmed' later?
In rare cases called "Chain Reorganizations," 1 or 2 confirmations can be undone if two miners find blocks at nearly the same time. This is why businesses wait for at least **3 confirmations** before shipping a product.

### 3. Does 1 BTC confirm faster than 0.001 BTC?
No. The speed of confirmation is determined by the **Fee Rate (sat/vB)** and pure luck, not the amount of Bitcoin being sent. Miners care about the fee you pay them, not the price of the coins.
