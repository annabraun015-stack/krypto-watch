## Section 7: FAQ — Navigating the Market

### 1. What is the best exchange for beginners?
In 2026, the most reliable platforms for new users are **Coinbase**, **Kraken**, and **Bitpanda** (for European users). These platforms are highly regulated, insured, and offer simple "Buy" buttons for your first transaction.

### 2. Can I buy just a fraction of a Bitcoin?
Absolutely. Most people in 2026 do not own an entire Bitcoin. You can buy as little as **$10 worth of BTC**. Bitcoin is divisible down to eight decimal places, so you can own exactly as much or as little as you want.

### 3. What is KYC?
KYC stands for **"Know Your Customer."** It is a legal requirement for regulated exchanges to verify your identity. You will usually need to provide a photo of your passport or ID and a "selfie" to prove you are who you say you are.

### 4. Is it safe to link my bank account?
Yes, on top-tier exchanges. These platforms use the same security standards as your bank. However, we always recommend using **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**—specifically an app like Google Authenticator—to protect your account.

### 5. How do I avoid high fees?
The "Buy Now" buttons on many apps charge a premium for convenience. Once you are comfortable, use the **"Advanced Trading"** section of the exchange. By placing a "Limit Order" instead of a "Market Order," you can often reduce your fees by 50% or more.
