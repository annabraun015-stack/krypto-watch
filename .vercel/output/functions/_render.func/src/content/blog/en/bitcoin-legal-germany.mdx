## Legal Status of Bitcoin in Germany

**Yes — Bitcoin is legal in Germany.**

Germany is one of the most advanced countries in the world in terms of cryptocurrency regulation. Bitcoin has been classified by the Federal Financial Supervisory Authority (BaFin) as a **unit of account** — a sub-form of financial instruments according to § 1 Para. 11 KWG (Banking Act).

This means: Bitcoin is not legal tender like the Euro, but it is perfectly legal to:
- Buy and sell Bitcoin
- Own and store Bitcoin
- Accept Bitcoin as payment
- Trade and safeguard Bitcoin (with BaFin permission for companies)

> **Featured Snippet:** Bitcoin is completely legal in Germany. BaFin classifies Bitcoin as a unit of account (financial instrument). German citizens can buy, own, trade, and sell Bitcoin. Gains from the sale of Bitcoin are tax-free after a holding period of one year. For companies offering Bitcoin services, a BaFin license is required.

## Bitcoin as a Unit of Account

BaFin classified Bitcoin as a **unit of account** in 2013 — one of the most important regulatory classifications in Europe at that time.

**What does unit of account mean?**

A unit of account is a medium of exchange that can be accepted by private individuals but is not legal tender. Bitcoin is therefore treated similarly to foreign currencies in Germany — it can be used but has no statutory exchange rate.

**Practical consequences:**
- Merchants CAN accept Bitcoin, but they DON'T HAVE TO
- Bitcoin transactions are subject to accounting requirements for companies
- Profits from Bitcoin sales are taxable (with exceptions)

First, understand [what Bitcoin basically is](/en/blog/what-is-bitcoin/) before you dive deeper into the German legal situation.

## Regulation by BaFin

**BaFin (Federal Financial Supervisory Authority)** is the central regulatory authority for the financial sector in Germany.

### What BaFin Regulates

| Activity | BaFin Required | Legal Basis |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Private Bitcoin buying/selling | No | — |
| Operating a Bitcoin exchange | Yes — §32 KWG Permission | KWG, VAG |
| Bitcoin custody (Custody) | Yes — Crypto custody business | §1 Para. 1a KWG |
| Offering Bitcoin funds | Yes — Capital management company | KAGB |
| Operating a Bitcoin ATM | Yes — Money transfer business | ZAG |
| Private mining | No (generally) | — |

Since **2020**, the **crypto custody business** has been classified as a regulated financial business in Germany. Companies that store Bitcoin for third parties explicitly require BaFin permission.

### Leading German Crypto Companies with BaFin License

- **Coinbase Germany GmbH** — BaFin-licensed platform
- **Deutsche Börse Digital** — Institutional trading platform
- **Bitwala (now Nuri)** — Banking + Bitcoin (licensed)
- **Coinfinity** — Austrian, EU-regulated
- **Bison** — App from Stuttgart Stock Exchange (BaFin-certified)

## Bitcoin and Taxes in Germany

This is the **most important section** for German Bitcoin users. The tax regulation is clear, but not always immediately intuitive.

### Base Rule: Private Disposal Transactions (§23 EStG)

Bitcoin gains are subject to **income tax law** in Germany as private disposal transactions.

| Situation | Tax |
|-----------|--------|
| Bitcoin sold and profit &lt; **600 Euro** | Tax-free (exemption limit) |
| Bitcoin held for **less than 1 year** and sold | Taxable (personal income tax rate) |
| Bitcoin held for **more than 1 year** and sold | **Tax-free!** |

### The Tax Efficiency Principle: FIFO

The tax office usually uses the **FIFO principle** (First In, First Out): The first Bitcoins bought are considered the first sold. This is important for calculating the 1-year holding period.

---

## One-Year Holding Period: The Most Important Tax Rule

The **one-year holding period** (sometimes called the "speculation period") is the cornerstone of German Bitcoin taxation:

```
Example Calculation:
Purchase: 0.5 BTC on 01.03.2024 for 30,000 Euro
Sale: 0.5 BTC on 05.04.2025 for 50,000 Euro
Holding time: 13 months → MORE than 1 year
Profit: 20,000 Euro
Tax: TAX-FREE ✓

Purchase: 0.5 BTC on 01.03.2024 for 30,000 Euro
Sale: 0.5 BTC on 15.11.2024 for 50,000 Euro
Holding time: 8.5 months → LESS than 1 year
Profit: 20,000 Euro
Tax: Personal tax rate (25-45%) on 20,000 Euro
     → Tax burden: approx. 5,000–9,000 Euro!
```

**Note:** The 1-year rule is a huge advantage for long-term Bitcoin investors ("HODLers") in Germany. Anyone who buys Bitcoin and holds it for at least 1 year pays no capital gains tax — in contrast to stock gains, which are always taxable.

## Taxable Bitcoin Events

| Event | Taxable? |
|----------|----------------|
| Buying Bitcoin (EUR → BTC) | No |
| Selling Bitcoin (BTC → EUR) | Yes (if &lt; 1 year) |
| Trading Bitcoin (BTC → ETH) | Yes (if &lt; 1 year, counts as sale) |
| Spending Bitcoin for purchases | Yes (if &lt; 1 year, counts as sale) |
| Mining Bitcoin | Yes (Income tax on market value at receipt) |
| Receiving Bitcoin as payment | Yes (Income tax on market value) |
| Receiving Bitcoin as gift | No (Gift tax applies at high amounts) |
| Inheriting Bitcoin | No (Inheritance tax separate) |

**Important:** Crypto-to-crypto exchange (e.g., BTC → ETH) is treated as a sale for tax purposes. The 1-year period starts anew!

## EU MiCA Regulation

Since 2024, the **MiCA Regulation (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)** has applied EU-wide, including Germany.

### What MiCA Means for Bitcoin Users

| Area | MiCA Impact |
|---------|----------------|
| Crypto exchanges | Must be licensed EU-wide (CASP license) |
| Stablecoins | Strict reserve requirements (USDT restrictions) |
| Advertising | Risk information mandatory |
| Private investors | Hardly any direct impact |
| Wallet providers | Some must be registered |

**Bitcoin itself** is largely exempt from MiCA, as it is decentralized and not issued by an issuer. The regulation is primarily aimed at centrally issued tokens and stablecoins.

## What is Prohibited?

Although Bitcoin is legal in Germany, there are clear prohibitions:

❌ **Operating an unlicensed crypto exchange** — Commercial trading is prohibited without BaFin permission

❌ **Money laundering with Bitcoin** — §261 StGB applies fully to Bitcoin transactions

❌ **Tax evasion** — Failing to report Bitcoin gains is tax evasion

❌ **Ponzi schemes with Bitcoin** — Fraud remains fraud, regardless of the medium of exchange

❌ **Unlicensed offering of investment advice** — Bitcoin investment advice without a license is prohibited

## Practical Tips for German Bitcoin Investors

### Tax Recommendations

1. **Always document purchase and sale dates** — Date, quantity, price, exchange
2. **Use FIFO software** — Tools like CoinTracking, Blockpit, or Koinly automate tax calculation
3. **Annex SO at the tax office** — Bitcoin gains are explained in the "Other Income" annex of form SO
4. **Observe exemption limits** — Up to 600 Euro profit per year is tax-free
5. **Tax advisor with crypto expertise** — Important for more complex situations (mining, lending, DeFi)

### Simplified Tax Process

```
Bitcoin Tax Annual Process:
Jan: Export all purchase/sale data from the previous year
     ↓
Feb: Import into tax software (CoinTracking/Blockpit)
     ↓
Mar: FIFO calculation of taxable profits
     ↓
Apr: Fill out Annex SO with calculated profits
     ↓
Jul: Submit tax return (regular deadline)
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is it legal to own Bitcoin in Germany?
Yes, completely legal. Private individuals can own any amount of Bitcoin. There is no reporting requirement for simply owning Bitcoin.

### Do I have to report Bitcoin gains to the tax office?
Yes, if you sold Bitcoin at a profit within 12 months and the total profit exceeds 600 Euro. For holding periods over 1 year, profits are tax-free, but it is still recommended to document the transactions.

### Can I accept Bitcoin as a payment method in Germany?
Yes — companies can accept Bitcoin as a payment method. However, the income must be booked as turnover at the market value at the time of receipt.

### Do I need a license to buy Bitcoin?
No. Private individuals do not need a license. Only companies providing Bitcoin services for third parties (custody, trading, investment advice) require BaFin permission.

### How are Bitcoin gains taxed in Germany?
At the personal income tax rate (14–45%) if sold within 12 months. For holding periods over 12 months: Full tax exemption.

### What happens if I forget to report Bitcoin?
The tax office increasingly has access to data from crypto exchanges. Tax evasion can lead to back payments plus interest plus fines. In the case of active non-reporting, criminal prosecution is possible.

### Is Bitcoin mining legal in Germany?
Yes, but mining income is subject to income tax. Commercial mining can also be subject to trade tax.

### Which German crypto exchanges are reputable?
**Bitcoin.de** (largest German P2P platform, BaFin-regulated), **Bison** (Stuttgart Stock Exchange), **Bitpanda** (EU-licensed), **Coinbase** (BaFin-certified). Use only licensed platforms.
